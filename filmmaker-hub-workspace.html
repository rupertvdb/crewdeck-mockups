Design rationale · Studio Desk

Spatial bet. 220px sidebar, ~640–760px canvas, no right rail. The sidebar is navigation furniture, not a hierarchy — slim enough to ignore, present enough that ⌘K beats it for muscle memory. The canvas runs single-column because a returning user reads top-down, not scans a grid.

Rejected. A two-column "projects + today" split (fragments attention), tile cards for projects (cards are for browsing, lists are for working), and a hero greeting with the time of day (sentimental, not load-bearing).

Signature move. The Next rail — a single accent-bordered row that sits between Pick-up and Today and answers the one question Rupert actually has when he sits down: "what do I open first?" One row. One primary key. The blue-bar marker is the only painted accent in the whole canvas; everything else earns the eye through hairlines, weight, and a single status dot.
